About

I'm Henry Pham Duc — a 50-something tennis enthusiast in Surrey, BC, and a long-time tai chi practitioner. I research the biomechanics of the modern tennis groundstroke, the kinetic chain, and the surprisingly deep overlap between fascial lines, the stretch-shortening cycle, and traditional Chinese movement arts.

Tôi là Henry Phạm Đức — người đam mê môn tennis ở Surrey, BC, và người tập Taichi đã lâu. Tôi nghiên cứu cơ sinh học cú thuận tay hiện đại, chuỗi động học, và sự giao thoa đáng ngạc nhiên giữa các đường fascia, chu trình kéo-rút ngắn, với các môn võ-thuật truyền thống Trung Hoa.

These pages are the home base for what I publish: Tennis for Every One (my long-running coaching blog), a bilingual Tennis Wiki (Vietnamese & English), a parallel Tai Chi Wiki, an AI coach, a biomechanics research lab, and a free bilingual ebook library.

Why tennis AND tai chi?

Tennis teaches me speed, power, and the kinetic chain. Tai chi teaches me root, fascia, and what happens after the kinetic chain ends. Both are conversations with the ground. Both are about how a human body organizes force from the floor up. The crossover research lives in both wikis — and is the most interesting work I do.
